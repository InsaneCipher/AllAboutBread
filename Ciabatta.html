Ciabatta

Ciabatta is an Italian white bread made by a baker in Adria, province of Rovigo, Veneto, Italy, in response to the popularity of French baguettes.

Ciabatta is somewhat elongated, broad, and flat, and is baked in many variations, although unique for its alveolar holes.

Ciabatta bread was first produced in 1982.
