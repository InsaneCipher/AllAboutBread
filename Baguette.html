Baguette

A baguette is a long, thin type of bread of French origin that is commonly made from basic lean dough.

The word baguette means "wand" "baton" or "stick". It was first recorded as a kind of bread in 1920.

Algeria consumes about 49 million baguettes per day and France consumes about 30 million.
