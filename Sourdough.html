Sourdough

Sourdough bread is made by the fermentation of dough using wild bacteria and yeast. The lactic acid from the fermentaion creates a sour taste and improves how long the bread lasts.

The oldest Sourdough bread was excavated in Switzerland and dates back to around 3700 BC.

Sourdough bread is a part of San Francisco culture as it was brought to Northern California by French bakers during the California Gold Rush.
