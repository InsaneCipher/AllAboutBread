Naan Bread

Naan is an oven-baked flatbread which is found mainly found in the cuisine of the Indian subcontinent but is also found in Western Asia, Central Asia, Indonesia, Malaysia, Myanmar, and the Caribbean.

The most familiar and readily available varieties of naan in Western countries are those from South Asia.

In South Asian cuisine, naans are typically flavored with fragrant essences, such as rosewater, khus, or with butter or ghee melted on them. Nigella seeds are commonly added to naan as cooked in Indian, Bangladeshi and Pakistani restaurants.
