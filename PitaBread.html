Pita Bread

Pita or pitta is a family of yeast-leavened round flatbreads common in the Mediterranean, Middle East, and neighboring areas.

During the Stone Age the Natufian people in what is now Jordan made a kind of flatbread from wild cereal grains.

Modern commercial pita bread is prepared on advanced automatic lines. These lines have high production capacities, processing 100,000-pound (45,000 kg) silos of flour at a time and producing thousands of loaves per hour.
